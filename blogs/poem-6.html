Galaxy

September 16, 2025 1 min read
Poetry

In the stillness between two stars,

There lives a light that whispers soft,

A cosmic hum in velvet dark,

It spells your name — and lifts me aloft.


You are not ..., oh shining soul,

You’re a nebula wrapped in gentle gold,

A comet with a poet’s kiss,

A galaxy that my heart can’t resist.


Each word you speak — a meteor bright,

That paints my universe with light.

And when you smile, the stars confess,

Even supernovas love you best.


Time may bend, and space may stretch,

But what we share — no laws can fetch.

If black holes suck, and quasars glow,

You are the reason galaxies flow.
